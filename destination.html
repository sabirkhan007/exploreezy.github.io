AULI

Auli is a hill station in the Chamoli District famous fir it’s Himalayan skiing resort. It is surrounded by oak forests, the Nanda Devi and Nar Parvat mountains. It is also known as Auli Bugyals which means Meadows in Garhwali. A long cable car links Auli to the town of Joshimath. Auli is India’s premier ski resort destination. Mainly developed as a paramilitary base, Auli’s skiing slopes are very popular and attracts a lot of tourists and professionals alike. In winter, Auli hosts several snow adventure events.

NAINITAL

Nainital is a beautiful place that promises to calm your senses, commonly known as the ‘Lake District’, It is a famous hill station in Uttarakhand, famous for it’s breath-taking beauty, lush green grasslands, mesmerising landscapes, emerald lakes, and ancient temples and various adventure activitie. This beautiful town in surrounded by seven hills, popularly known as ‘Sapta-Shring’ – Ayarpata, Deopata, Handi-Bandi, Naina, Alma, Lariya-Kanta and Sher-Ka-Danda. The place is very popular for newly married couples who visit here for Hani moon. Visiters can paddle in the lake or even try riding a horse in the town. Nainital is also popular for hot-air ballooning and mountaineering.

The is interesting fact about these lakes is that they are all freshwater lakes, and they are interconnected to each other. NEW TEHRI

It is a modern town and the headquarter of the Tehri-Garhwal district, New Tehri is an adventure tourism hub. Overlooking the massive Tehri Lake and dam, the well-planned town attracts the tourists who come here for its natural beauty and the opportunity to enjoy water sports in the lake. It is Surrounded by beautiful temples and dense forests, and set against beautiful Himalayan peaks, New Tehri is an ideal vacation spot. Here different adventure events are organised throughout the year and the most popular one is “ Tehri Lake Festival” . Tehri dam is the highest of its kind in India and is regarded among the world’s largest hydroelectric projects stand on the Bhagirathi river.

RISHIKESH

It about 24kms away from Haridwar, Located in the laps of lower Himalayas, and is surrounded by scenic beauty of the hills on three sides with Holy Ganga flowing through it. The whole place is considered to be sacred as it is believed that meditation at this place leads to attainment of salvation. There are many temples-some ancient, some new along the river Ganges. Rishikesh is important not only as pilgrimage center closely associated with the Ramayana, but also as home for many important centers of Hindu religious thought and spirituality, and as a very great Yoga Centre, Rishikesh also known as the “ Yoga capital of World ”.There are a great many ashrams and Yoga institutes here, it is also famous for many adventure activities such as River Rafting, Kayaking, cliff Jumping , Rock climbing etc. Rishikesh is also the gateway and the start-off point for journeys to important religious places like Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri.

DHANAULTI

Dhanaulti is located at an altitude of 2,250 m, it is settled very far away from the hustle bustle, busy market places and pollution. GARTANGALI

Gartangali is one of the most thrilling and exciting treks located in the beautiful Nelong Valley near the Indo-China border,in Uttarkashi district, The 150-year old Gartang Gali is a 136-m-long wood-lined stairway that is said to have been built by Peshawari Pathans, who created an architectural masterpiece by cutting the granite of a vertical rock face. This wooden bridge was the trade route between India and Tibet. The nomads (Bhotiya tribe) ferried their goods on yaks from this bridge for barter with their other country part. It was damaged over the years after it fell into disuse following the Indo China war in 1962. But Uttarakhand government decided to reconstruct it and finally it opened for the visitors and tourists after 59 years in 2021.
