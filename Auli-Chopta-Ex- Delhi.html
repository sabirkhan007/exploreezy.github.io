Overview

Haridwar is the beginning point of Chopta and Auli. First stop on this tour is Chopta, it is quite famous a for its peaceful Himalayan peaks and breath-taking views in the Country, and the tour ends at Auli it is also famous for it’s skiing resorts. Also remember, following are the sequence which the tourists need to follow during the trip. This 04 night and 05 days adventure tour from Haridwar offers the sightseeing of breath-taking and memorable tourist attractions with comfortable accommodations and facilities in between the tour.

06 Nights/07 Days

Itinerary

Day 01: DELHI – HARIDWAR (230kms/5-6HRS)


    • On Arrival Delhi Airport / Delhi Railway Station, our driver will pick you further drive to Haridwar. On arrival check in at the hotel. After fresh up visit Mansa Devi and perform evening Ganga Aarti at Har ki Pauri. Dinner and Overnight stay at the hotel.

      Pick up from Delhi

      Visit Mansa Devi Temple (if time permits)

      Visit Har-ki-Pauri

Day 02: HARIDWAR – CHOPTA (200 Kms. 6-7 Hrs) Height: 2700 meters.


    • Morning after breakfast, our driver will pick you up from Haridwar then smooth drive to Chopta, Enroute you can see the beautiful Mountains of Garhwal Region. On the way you can also visit deoria tal it’s one of the beautiful lake in chopta Check in at the hotel after arrival in Chopta. Dinner and overnight stay at Chopta.

    • Chopta: Chopta is famous for it’s breath-taking beauty and lush green grasslands, it is also known as Mini Switzerland because of its snow capped mountains and meadows. Chopta is a part of Kedarnath wildlife sanctuary which is rich in flora and fauna with dense forests around and a variety of Rhododendron and Deodar trees. Tourists can see heart warming peaceful 360-degree view of the mountain ranges like Chaukhamba, Trishul and Nanda Devi from here.

      Drive to Chopta

      Visit Deoria Tal

      Stay at Chopta

Day 3: CHOPTA – TUNGNATH – CHOPTA (4km Trek one way) Height: 3,690 meters


    • Early morning start your trek for Tungnath which is 4 kms away from Chopta after. It will take few hours to reach Tungnath temple. It doesn’t matter you are Religious or non-religious if you want to feel the vibes just sit in the courtyard of the temple for a few minutes with eyes closed you will feel very light. After puja and Darshan relax for sometime in the peaceful environment then proceed for Chandrashilla which 1km ahead from Tungnath. The efforts you put for trekking will be rewarded multiple times with views which are surely breathtaking. From the Chandrashila point you can see big Himalayan peaks (Nanda Devi, Trishul, Chaukhamba and many more) and other surrounding valleys in all directions. spend some time in the beautiful environment and then in the evening return back to your hotel. Dinner and overnight stay at hotel.

    • Tungnath: Tungnath is one of the highest Shiva temples in the world and also it is the highest among the five Panch Kedar temples located in the Rudraprayag district, According to vedas Tungnath means “Lord of the peaks”. The Temple was built by the Pandavas at this place to worship Lord Shiva and please him. According to mythology the Lord Shiva's Arms were found here by Pandavas when they were finding him to attain the Moksha.

      Start your trek for Tungnath

      Pooja and Darshan Tungnath Temple.

      Visit Chandrashila

      Back to Chopta.

Day 4: CHOPTA – AULI (150 kms/5-6 Hrs) Height: 2,800 metres.


    • Morning after breakfast proceed to Joshimath, then take the cable car from Joshimath to Auli. On Arrival check in at the hotel, after fresh up visit Auli artificial lake, gurso bugyal and more places.Dinner and overnight stay at hotel.

    • Auli: Auli is a hill station in the Chamoli District famous fir it’s Himalayan skiing resort. It is surrounded by oak forests, the Nanda Devi and Nar Parvat mountains. It is also as Auli Bugyals which means Meadows in Garhwali. A long cable car links Auli to the town of Joshimath.

      Start your trek for Tungnath

      Proceed to Joshimath

      Enjoy Cable Car from Joshimath to Auli

      Enjoy Local Sightseeing at Auli ( if time Permits)

      Overnight stay at Auli

Day 5: AULI – RUDRAPRAYAG (100 kms/3-4Hrs)


    • Morning after breakfast, enjoy full day at Auli by enjoying snow skiing and other activities, later in the evening proceed to Rudraprayag, On arrival check in at the hotel. Dinner and Overnight stay at hotel.

    • Joshimath is situated on the slopes above the confluence of the rivers Alaknanda and Dhauliganga. Of the four ‘Maths’ established by Adi Shankaracharya, Pipalkothi is the winter seat of Badrinath. The idol is brought down to Pipalkothi and installed in the temple for people to worship. There are many other temples in the township. The most important is the temple of Nir Singh with the idol of Lord Vishnu. The left arm of this deity is getting destroyed with time and the popular belief holds that the day the arm completely withers Badrinath valley will cease to exist and the Gods will transfer the residence into the neighboring Niti Valley at Bhavishya Badri.

      Enjoy Full Day at Auli

      Proceed to Rudraprayag

      Stay at Rudraprayag

Day 6: RUDRAPRYAG – HARIDWAR (140 kms/5-6Hrs)


    • Morning after breakfast, bid farewell to your Auli & Chopta tour and drop to Haridwar via Rishikesh.

    • RISHIKESH: 24 Kms. From Hardwar. Located in the laps of lower Himalayas, and is surrounded by scenic beauty of the hills on three sides with Holy Ganga flowing through it. The whole place is considered to be sacred as it is believed that meditation at this place leads to attainment of salvation. There are many temples-some ancient, some new along the river Ganges. Rishikesh is important not only as pilgrimage center closely associated with the Ramayana, but also as home for many important centers of Hindu religious thought and spirituality, and as a very great Yoga Centre. There are a great many ashrams and Yoga institutes here. Rishikesh is also the gateway and the start-off point for journeys to important religious places like Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri.
